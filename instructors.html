Meet Our Instructors

Leticia Fererra

Leticia found yoga on a whim thanks to a Friend’s gift of a free month of yoga. She went and was immediately hooked. One of the biggest benefits he received was being able to fully let go of her stressful workday through practice. “That was something I could not get doing other exercise activities. After just a couple of months I knew Yoga was something I wanted to have a bigger presence in my life. So I decided to become a teacher.” In Spring 2014, Amy completed yoga Teacher Training.

Keila Bolanos

Keila has been teaching yoga since 1996. She also brings with her knowledge and experience gained from 20 years of practicing massage therapy. She began teaching yoga when she realized how much it would benefit her clients. Her deep interest and knowledge of the energy system of our bodies gives her a unique awareness of the profound affect yoga has on the health of our bodies. She has a deep respect for the power of yoga to enhance our core strength.

Kara Thorson

Kara is a Certified Yoga Teacher and Founder of The Little yoga Room in Montreal, has been practicing yoga since 1992 and strictly Vinyasa style for the last 12 years. She has studied in India with the top yogis. Yoga is really the art of waking up. Getting back to the true you. It can be that simple. Yoga offers up a way for us to see a world that is working for you instead of against you. Yoga reminds me that everything is connected so we must live, act, dance, breathe with awareness. If the journey really is the reward, then by golly I choose to enJOY the journey. I hope you will join me

Alex Tomkins

Alex found yoga while she was struggling with depression. Yoga taught her to slow down and breathe, to take responsibility for her life, mindfulness, awareness, and self-love. She says, "It showed me that I created my own reality by choosing which thoughts and events I gave my life force and attention to. I was drawn to yoga for the philosophy side of things and my teaching is definitely influenced by that. I strongly believe that yoga is a spiritual practice; the physical benefits are just a byproduct. I am deeply passionate about introducing people to meditation as I believe there is nothing braver or sweeter than sitting with yourself in silence and stillness."
